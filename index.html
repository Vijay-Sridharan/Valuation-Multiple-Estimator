Assumptions & Limitations
1 · No Leverage
Assumes Net Debt = 0, so Equity Value ≈ Enterprise Value. The PE anchor is an equity multiple; EV/Revenue and EV/GMV are enterprise multiples. Valid for equity-funded early-stage companies. Adjust for leveraged businesses.
2 · No Minority Interests
Treats the entity as 100% owned. Where significant subsidiaries are not wholly owned, the multiple will overstate value attributable to the parent. Use a sum-of-parts approach instead.
3 · Steady-State PAT Margin
Enter the normalised at-scale margin, not the current one. Current losses will produce undefined multiples. Think of this as the terminal-year margin in a DCF — what the business earns once growth moderates.
4 · Financial Services
Not directly applicable to banks or NBFCs — revenue is a function of leverage, making EV/Revenue unreliable. Use PE directly (Revenue = PAT, all margins at 100%). For P/BV: enter Loan Book as GTV and NIM as take rate. Using equity ratio as take rate works algebraically but makes PAT margin mean ROE — use with care.
Methodology: PEG = 1.0, anchored at PE 20× for 20% PAT growth on listed market comps. For GMV, EV/GMV = take rate × PAT% × PE. For Gross Margin, EV/GP = (PAT% ÷ GM%) × PE. Narrative factor applies as a qualitative overlay. Estimates are indicative only and do not constitute investment advice or a formal valuation.
